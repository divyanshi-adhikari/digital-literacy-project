# Cybercrime Case Study: The UPI/Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime in India where attackers 
trick users into authorizing fraudulent transactions. This type of fraud has increased 
rapidly with the growing use of digital payments. In a typical case, the fraudster 
contacts the victim through a phone call, SMS, or messaging apps like WhatsApp and 
pretends to be a bank official, customer care representative, or buyer/seller. 
The attacker then sends a “collect request” on a UPI app, falsely claiming it is 
required to receive money. The victim, unaware of how UPI works, enters their UPI PIN, 
which actually approves a payment instead of receiving it. In some cases, fraudsters 
also use fake links or QR codes that redirect users to phishing websites.

Riya, a college student who had recently started using digital payments, became a victim 
of a cleverly executed UPI fraud. After cancelling an online order, she expected a refund 
within a few days. Instead, she received a call from a person claiming to be a customer 
support executive. The caller sounded professional and even mentioned her purchase 
details, which made the situation seem genuine. He assured her that the refund could be 
processed instantly if she followed a few simple steps on her UPI app. Trusting him, 
Riya opened her app and received a “collect request.” Believing it was needed to accept 
the refund, she entered her UPI PIN. Within seconds, ₹4,999 was deducted from her account, 
and the caller disconnected. When she tried to contact him again, the number was switched 
off, leaving her confused and distressed.

This type of fraud commonly targets students, elderly individuals, and people who are 
new to digital payment systems, as they may not fully understand how transaction 
authorization works. The consequences include financial loss, emotional stress, and 
reduced trust in online systems. This case highlights the importance of digital 
awareness. Users must remember that entering a UPI PIN always means money is being 
sent, not received. Being cautious, verifying unknown requests, and avoiding suspicious 
links are essential steps to prevent such cybercrimes.
